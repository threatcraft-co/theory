## 1. Nature of the Tool

THEORY is an open-source threat actor intelligence aggregation framework. It
collects, normalizes, and synthesizes publicly available information from
third-party sources including but not limited to MITRE ATT&CK, Malpedia,
AlienVault OTX, SigmaHQ, ThreatFox, CISA, and vendor research blogs.

THEORY does not produce original threat intelligence research. All data
surfaced by THEORY originates from external sources that Threatcraft Co. does
not own, control, maintain, or independently verify. Threatcraft Co. makes no
claim of ownership over any data ingested, processed, or displayed by this
tool.

---

## 2. Intended Use

THEORY is designed for use by:

- Threat intelligence analysts and researchers
- Detection engineers and security operations professionals
- Penetration testers and red team operators engaged in **authorized**
  security assessments (see Section 3)
- Students and academics studying cybersecurity
- Organizations conducting defensive security operations

This tool is intended to support the security community's collective
understanding of threat actors, their tactics, techniques, and procedures
(TTPs), and associated indicators of compromise (IOCs).

THEORY optionally uses large language model (LLM) APIs to synthesize vendor
